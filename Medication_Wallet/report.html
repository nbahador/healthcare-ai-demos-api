Original Medication Image

Ibuprofen

Form
Tablet, Capsule, Liquid, Chewable tablet
Strength
100mg, 200mg, 400mg, 600mg, 800mg
Therapeutic Class
Nonsteroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug (NSAID)
Primary Use
Relief of mild to moderate pain, fever reduction, and inflammation associated with conditions such as arthritis, menstrual cramps, and headaches.

Dosage Information

Recommended Dosage
Adults: 200-400mg every 4-6 hours as needed, not to exceed 1200mg in 24 hours. Children: 5-10mg/kg every 6-8 hours as needed, not to exceed 40mg/kg in 24 hours.
Instructions for Use
Take with food or milk to minimize stomach upset. Swallow tablets or capsules whole, do not crush or chew. Shake liquid well before use.

Safety Information

Storage Instructions
Store at room temperature, away from moisture and heat. Do not freeze.
Signs of Overdose
Signs of overdose may include: nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, drowsiness, and confusion. In severe cases, may lead to seizures, coma, and death.

Potential Side Effects

  • Stomach upset, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, headache, dizziness, and fatigue.
  • Rare but serious side effects: stomach ulcers, bleeding, kidney damage, and liver damage.

Drug Interactions

This medication may interact with:

  • Aspirin: Increased risk of stomach ulcers and bleeding.
  • Blood thinners (e.g., warfarin): Increased risk of bleeding.
  • Diuretics: Decreased effectiveness of diuretics.
  • Lithium: Increased risk of lithium toxicity.
  • Methotrexate: Increased risk of methotrexate toxicity.

Generic Alternatives

  • Advil
  • Motrin
  • Nuprin
  • Ibuprofen (generic)
